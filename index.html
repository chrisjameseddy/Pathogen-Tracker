Primary source — the majority of this table (organism list, incubation ranges, symptoms, implicated foods) was transcribed from a photographed page headed "Appendix 5. State Government of Victoria, Australia, Department of Health. health.vic.gov.au

Cross-checked against, and used to fill gaps not visible in the photos (Bacillus cereus split, Giardia, Entamoeba histolytica, and the "key feature"/complication notes):

  • CDC — Food Poisoning Symptoms table, cdc.gov/food-safety/signs-symptoms
  • NSW Food Authority — Foodborne illness pathogens table, foodauthority.nsw.gov.au
  • CDC DPDx — Giardiasis, cdc.gov/dpdx/giardiasis
  • CDC — About Giardia Infection, cdc.gov/giardia/about
  • CDC DPDx — Cryptosporidiosis, cdc.gov/dpdx/cryptosporidiosis, and CDC Symptoms of Crypto, cdc.gov/cryptosporidium/signs-symptoms
  • CDC — Amebiasis, cdc.gov/amebiasis/about
  • CDC — Symptoms of Listeria Infection, cdc.gov/listeria/signs-symptoms; Angelo KM et al., "Assessment of the Incubation Period for Invasive Listeriosis," Clin Infect Dis 2016;63(11):1487–1489 (median 11 days; commonly cited range 3–70 days), PMC5421300
  • WHO EMRO — Cholera, emro.who.int; MSD Manual Professional Edition — Cholera, msdmanuals.com

Where the photographed table and these sources gave slightly different ranges, the photographed document (your coursework source) was kept as the primary figure, since it's presumably the version you're being assessed against — differences were generally minor (e.g. exact upper bound of a range).

Compiled field reference for personal study use — not a substitute for local public health guidance, lab confirmation, or clinical judgement. Ranges are typical/commonly cited and individual cases vary. See Sources section above for full citations.
